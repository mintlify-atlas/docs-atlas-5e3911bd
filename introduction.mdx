# AI Basketball Referee

The AI Basketball Referee is a cutting-edge computer vision system that brings automated officiating to basketball games. Using custom-trained YOLO (You Only Look Once) models and pose estimation, it detects basketball violations in real-time with the same precision sports like tennis and track & field have achieved.

## How it works

The system combines two powerful computer vision techniques to analyze basketball gameplay:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Basketball detection">
    A custom YOLO model trained on 3,000 annotated images detects basketballs in each video frame. The model predicts bounding boxes around detected basketballs and tracks their position and movement over time.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Player pose estimation">
    YOLO pose estimation identifies and tracks keypoints on players' bodies, including ankles, knees, hips, elbows, and wrists. These keypoints enable accurate tracking of player movements.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Violation detection">
    By analyzing the relationship between ball position and player keypoints across consecutive frames, the system applies predefined rules to detect travels and double dribbles.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Real-time feedback">
    Violations are highlighted on the video feed with visual indicators, making it easy for referees and users to review calls. The system also generates logs for further analysis.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Basketball detection model

The core of the basketball detection system is a custom-trained YOLO model (`basketballModel.pt`) that has learned to recognize basketballs across various conditions:

- **3,000+ annotated images** covering diverse basketball poses, lighting conditions, and backgrounds
- **Real-time inference** processing video frames as they're captured
- **High confidence thresholds** (0.65) to minimize false positives
- **Bounding box predictions** that track ball position frame-by-frame

## Travel detection

Traveling occurs when a player takes steps without dribbling the ball. The system detects this by:

1. **Tracking ankle movement** - Monitors vertical displacement of left and right ankle keypoints
2. **Counting steps** - Increments step count when ankle displacement exceeds threshold (5 pixels)
3. **Monitoring dribbles** - Tracks ball vertical movement to detect dribble bounces
4. **Applying the rule** - Flags a travel when step count ≥ 2 while dribble count = 0

When a travel is detected:
- The frame gets a blue tint overlay
- "Travel Detected!" appears on screen
- The system saves 30 frames before and 60 frames after the violation for review
- Footage is automatically saved to the `travel_footage/` directory

## Double dribble detection

A double dribble violation happens when a player dribbles, holds the ball, then starts dribbling again. The detection logic:

1. **Wrist-to-ball proximity** - Calculates distance between ball center and player wrists
2. **Hold detection** - Flags holding when distance < 300 pixels for > 0.85 seconds
3. **Dribble tracking** - Monitors ball vertical motion to count new dribbles
4. **Violation trigger** - Detects double dribble when player held ball (`was_holding=true`) and starts a new dribble (`dribble_count > 0`)

Visual feedback includes:
- Blue tint while the ball is being held
- Red tint when a double dribble is detected
- "Double dribble!" message displayed for 3 seconds
- Real-time debug info showing ball position, wrist coordinates, and holding status

## Why this matters

Basketball has lagged behind other sports in adopting computer vision for officiating. Tennis uses Hawk-Eye for line calls, track & field uses photo finish cameras, but basketball still relies heavily on human judgment for calls that can change championship outcomes.

## Customizability and expansion

The system is designed to be highly customizable:

- **Detection thresholds** - Adjust confidence levels for ball and pose detection
- **Violation sensitivity** - Tune step thresholds, dribble thresholds, and hold duration
- **Video sources** - Use webcam (`cv2.VideoCapture(0)`) or video files (`cv2.VideoCapture('video.mp4')`)
- **Additional violations** - Extend the system to detect blocking fouls, carrying, and other violations

<Tip>
  The codebase includes experimental implementations for blocking foul detection, step counting, and dribble counting that you can build upon for your specific use case.
</Tip>
